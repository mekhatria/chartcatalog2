Objectives

There are many objectives to visualize a data set. Here are the main commonly used objectives:

  • Comparison
    The comparison is the process to estimate the similarities or not between two elements or more. Line charts, Bar charts, bubble chart, and tree maps are often used to compare categorical data.
  • Composition
    The composition is the nature of something’s ingredients or elements. Donut charts, pie charts, stacked bar charts, and stacked column charts are often used to visualize composition.
  • Distribution
    The distribution is how many times a variable appears either throughout time or space. Scatter, histograms, and pyramid charts are good choices to visualize distribution. These charts allow us to detect the frequency of the appearance of a variable at a glance.
  • Flow
    The flow is the action of moving along in a steady and continuous stream. Sankey diagram and Gantt are the main charts used to visualize the flow.
  • Hierarchy
    The hierarchy is a system where elements are arranged or ranked one above another. Treemap, sunburst, and circle chart are used to visualize hierarchy.
  • Hotspots
    The hot spot is an area with a significant activity such as extreme temperature, traffic jamming, etc.
  • Location
    The location usually refers to a geographic location. The main charts used to visualize locations are maps charts.
  • Relationship
    The data relationship is how a variable’s behavior affects other variables. One of the powerful charts to find relationship are scatter and bubble charts, as they allow us to measure the relationship between two variables or more.
  • Trend
    A trend is a general direction or movement in which an element is developing or changing. A line chart and candlestick chart are used to visualize trends.
